Kalat county is located in northeastern of Iran, adjacent to Turkmenistan in the north by the hills of a relatively plain land that stretches one hundred and eighty kilometers, and from the south to the mountains of the Hezarmasjed and the city of Mashhad. From the east, it is limited to Sarakhs city and from the west to the Dargaz city. The distance from its center to Mashhad is 145 km with a relatively mountainous road

Negarestan Palace or Garden was built upon the order of Fath Ali Shah Qajar in the early 18th century. He had the plan to establish a governmental center in Tehran. Due to the existence of many paintings and murals remaining from Qajar dynasty, the palace was named Negarestan which means the place of paintings. At first, Negarestan Garden was very big. There were several halls and mansions in this garden such as Delgosha Mansion, Salam and Ghalamdan Halls.

The word Takht-e Jamshid in English is Persepolis which is a combination of Pérsēs and polis, and it means “The Persian city”. In Farsi, it is Takht-e Jamshid, which literally means “Throne of Jamshid”. It is believed that Jamshid; an Iranian mythological figure built the monument at the time of the Sassanid period.
